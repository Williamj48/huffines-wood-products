What is a cord?

A cord is the standard unit for measuring firewood. It is a tightly stacked pile that measures 4 feet tall, 4 feet wide, and 8 feet long — a total of 128 cubic feet. In practice, because of the air gaps between logs, you end up with roughly 80–90 cubic feet of actual wood. A full cord is a serious amount — enough to heat an average home for several months depending on your stove and how cold it gets.
